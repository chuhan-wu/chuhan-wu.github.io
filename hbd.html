Human Evalution System
When enters the 26th century, human society is centrally controlled by one political power which completely materialized human beings in the name of promoting social development. Throughout the processes of labeling individual citizen with barcode and evaluating the organs, central government tightly servile people’s daily behavior and their body condition.
[View my code on Github]

 


Background:

The HOE System is designed by engineers of the central government for cultivating talents by buying high-quality organs from citizens. With accurate calculation from HOE System, government builds outstanding army to reinforce their power and put down insurrections, and scientist groups to maintain high-speed technological development. The HOE System contains a camera detector and a user interface. Every single citizen is under the surveillance of Cameras that are located all over the city. When the camera detects human faces, it will automatically scan their barcodes and open up the HOE transaction system with their personal information.

 

In the transaction system, a 3D human model with the inside merchantable organs of the scanned person will display on the screen. People of central government may check each organ’s quality(usage out of 100%), condition(years of being used), and price(accurate market price) by simply clicking on the 3D model. The HOE System will also categorize each organ into three levels and show on the screen for government to make a selection on buying.

Description:

The HOE System combines openCV and UI design together to demonstrate a Utopia of the future world. I challenge the belief of human is superior to other species and have individual spirit. In the future world, human society may enter the period with centralized political power and expropriation of citizen’s freedom. Human is totally materialized instead of considered as superior species in the world. Their behavior will be servile and valued by price. By capturing human faces with a live camera that designed in science fiction, which includes diagrams, map, and data information, people who are scanned by the system will be placed in a simulated future environment. According to Grayson Cooke, the author of LIVENESS AND THE MACHINE - Improvisation in Live Audio-Visual Performance, a live performance has the great connection with its surrounding environment and artificial arrangement (Cooke 13).

 

Therefore, a well-designed environment is influential and can arouse users’ emotion by interacting with the system. Also, combining the openCV with a simulated future environment is another challenge. I was inspired by the Black Mirror and decided to expand on human detection system that was demonstrated by Professor Amy Alexander in 145A lecture. In Amy’s example, the camera will capture the human face and display a mark on it. I tried to add science fiction figures and data to the OpenCV and create the different barcode for each person.

Work Cited:

Press, Berkeley Electronic. “LIVENESS AND THE MACHINE - Improvisation in Live Audio-Visual Performance.” "Liveness and the Machine: Improvisation in Live Audio-Visual Performance" by Grayson Cooke, works.bepress.com/grayson_cooke/20/.
